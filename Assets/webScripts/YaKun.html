Our History

Ya Kun Kaya Toast began in 1944 when Loi Ah Koon started selling coffee, toast, and soft-boiled eggs from a small coffee stall in Tanjong Pagar. The name "Ya Kun" comes from his name - "Ya" meaning uncle in Hokkien, and "Kun" from Ah Koon.

The Founding Years

During the post-war period, Ah Koon's simple yet delicious breakfast offerings quickly became popular among local workers and residents. His secret kaya recipe, made with coconut milk, eggs, and pandan, was passed down through generations.

Family Legacy

The business was carried on by Ah Koon's son and later by his grandson, Adrin Loi, who modernized and expanded the brand while keeping the traditional recipes and methods intact. Today, Ya Kun maintains the same charcoal grilling technique and authentic kaya recipe that made it famous.

Modern Era

From a single stall, Ya Kun has grown into an international chain with outlets across Asia and beyond, becoming a symbol of Singapore's food heritage. Despite its growth, Ya Kun remains committed to preserving the authentic taste and experience that customers have loved for generations.

The Traditional Method

What makes Ya Kun special is not just the ingredients, but how they're prepared. The kaya is still made using the traditional slow-cooking method, stirred constantly over low heat for hours to achieve the perfect consistency. The bread is toasted over charcoal fires, giving it a unique texture and flavor that can't be replicated by modern toasters.
